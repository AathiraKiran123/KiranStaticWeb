About Me

Kiran Sreevalsan received his Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS) specializing in Pulse Diagnosis from Sri Sri College of Ayurvedic Science in Bangalore. He was head of the Sri Sri Ayurveda Center in Kerala for five years, where he was a much sought-after physician with sometimes 60 patients a day.

At the request of Sri Sri Ayurveda Netherlands he came to Europe where he gives consultations in the Netherlands, France, Belgium and Spain. In addition, he regularly gives lectures in which he explains clearly about the basic principles of Ayurveda. In 2019 he spoke by invitation at the European Parliament in Brussels and for the United Nations in Geneva.

Ayurveda

Ayurveda knowledge was passed on orally until it was written down more than 5,000 years ago. The oldest known texts about Ayurveda are the Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita and the Ashtanga Hrudaya. These texts detail the effects of the five elements in the cosmic system - earth, water, air, fire and space - on our individual system and explain the necessity of balancing these elements for a healthy and happy life. The power of good health lies in this balance achieved through the use of natural resources and principles.
